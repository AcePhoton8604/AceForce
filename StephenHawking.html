Stephen Hawking

Professor Stephen William Hawking was an English Theoretical Physicist , Cosmologist and author. He was the director at the Center for Theoretical Cosmology at the University of Cambridge at the time of his death.

Early Life

Hawking was born in Oxford on the 8th of January, 1942 to Frank and Isobel Hawking. Hawking had two younger sisters, Philippa and Mary, and an adopted brother, Edward Frank David. Hawking spent much of his later childhood in St. Albans , Hertfordshire

Work

Stephen Hawking had great contributions in many fields of Theoretical Physics. The most notable of such contributions includes (but is not limited to):

Hawking Radiation- It is the black-body radiation that is theorized to be released by black holes because of quantum effects near the black hole event horizon. It is the most well-known of Hawking's Contributions.
Black Hole Information Paradox The paradox that says that information is lost inside black holes, even though that is scientifically impossible.
Evidence of the Big Bang Theory Hawking mathematically proved that the Big Bang actually did happen.

Death

Professor Hawking had suffered from Motor Neurone Disease since the age of 21. Even though it's progress had considerably slowed down, it eventually did lead to his death. He died at his home in Cambridge on 14 th March 2018. He was 76 years old at the time.

Legacy

Hawking was a very popular person, and he left a huge impact on the world. Many of such contributions include (but are not limited to):

A Brief History of Time- A book dedicated to explaining the facts of science to the less science-oriented people.
Atheism- Hawking was an atheist and provided many scientific arguments and debates on the existence of god.
Predictions- Hawking predicted several things about the future of humanity; from artificial intelligence, to the end of the world.
Others- He was eulogised by figures in science, entertainment, politics, and other areas.
